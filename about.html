Machine Learning Research Lab

UNT MACHINE LEARNING LABMachine Learning Research Lab

UNT's Machine Learning Laboratory is dedicated to Natural Language Processing such as information

extraction, machine translation, sentiment analysis, question answering which might require both

syntactic and semantic analysis at various levels of abstractions.

Director

Dr. Cornelia Caragea is a computer scientist with expertise in

machine learning and data mining. She has developed and applied an abstraction-based approach to learning

compact classifiers from text and protein sequence data and researched the identification of important

sites in proteins using sequence information. She also has designed scalable algorithms for document and

citation recommendation in large digital libraries and studied subjectivity and sentiment analysis in

online forums. She was previously a postdoctoral scholar at Pennsylvania State University and a doctoral

assistant at Iowa State University. Her work has been published in leading data mining journals and

conference proceedings such as the Association for the Advancement of Artificial Intelligence, the

International Conference on Data Mining and BMC Bioinformatics.
